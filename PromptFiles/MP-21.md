# MP-21: Jinja2 Templates for Compliance Documents

## Goal
Create Jinja2 templates for all 5 compliance document types: privacy notice, consent text, retention matrix, breach SOP, and gap report. These templates are rendered with analysis data to produce downloadable documents.

## Dependencies
- MP-01 completed (`app/generation/` directory exists)

## Files to Create
- `backend/app/generation/templates/privacy_notice.jinja2`
- `backend/app/generation/templates/consent_text.jinja2`
- `backend/app/generation/templates/retention_matrix.jinja2`
- `backend/app/generation/templates/breach_sop.jinja2`
- `backend/app/generation/templates/gap_report.jinja2`

## Implementation

Create the `templates` directory:
```bash
mkdir -p backend/app/generation/templates

# PERSONAL DATA BREACH — INCIDENT RESPONSE SOP

*Per Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act, 2023 and Rule 6 of the DPDP Rules, 2025*

**Organization:** {{ company_name }}
**Data Protection Officer:** {{ dpo_name | default("[To Be Appointed]") }}
**Date:** {{ current_date }}

---

## 1. DETECTION & IDENTIFICATION

**Objective:** Identify and confirm the occurrence of a personal data breach.

1.1. Any employee who suspects a breach must immediately report to the DPO at {{ contact_email }}.
1.2. The DPO assesses whether the incident constitutes a "personal data breach" under the DPDP Act.
1.3. Log the initial report with: date/time of detection, reporter name, and preliminary description.

---

## 2. CONTAINMENT

**Objective:** Limit the scope and impact of the breach.

2.1. Isolate affected systems immediately.
2.2. Revoke compromised credentials/access tokens.
2.3. Preserve forensic evidence (do not delete logs).
2.4. Engage incident response team within 1 hour of confirmation.

---

## 3. ASSESSMENT

**Objective:** Determine the nature, scope, and severity of the breach.

3.1. Identify categories of personal data affected (identifiers, financial, health, children, etc.).
3.2. Estimate number of Data Principals affected.
3.3. Assess likely consequences for affected individuals.
3.4. Determine root cause.
3.5. Classify severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low.

---

## 4. NOTIFICATION — DATA PROTECTION BOARD

**Obligation:** Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act and Rule 6 of the DPDP Rules, 2025.

4.1. Notify the Data Protection Board of India **within the timeline prescribed under Rule 6**.
4.2. Notification must include:
   - Nature of the breach
   - Categories of personal data affected
   - Approximate number of Data Principals affected
   - Likely consequences of the breach
   - Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
4.3. Submit through the Board's designated portal.

---

## 5. NOTIFICATION — AFFECTED DATA PRINCIPALS

**Obligation:** Section 8(6) — notify each affected Data Principal.

5.1. Notify affected individuals **as soon as reasonably practicable**.
5.2. Notification must include:
   - Description of the breach in clear, plain language
   - Categories of their personal data affected
   - Likely consequences
   - Measures taken to address the breach
   - Steps they can take to protect themselves
   - Contact details of the DPO
5.3. Notification channels: email, SMS, in-app notification (use all available channels).

---

## 6. REMEDIATION

6.1. Implement technical fixes for the root cause.
6.2. Patch vulnerabilities, update access controls.
6.3. Conduct security review of related systems.
6.4. Update security safeguards per Rule 5.

---

## 7. DOCUMENTATION

7.1. Maintain a complete breach register with:
   - Incident timeline
   - Data categories and Data Principals affected
   - All notifications sent (Board + individuals)
   - Remediation actions taken
7.2. Retain records for audit purposes.

---

## 8. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

8.1. Conduct lessons-learned session within 14 days.
8.2. Update security policies and this SOP based on findings.
8.3. Conduct staff training on breach prevention.
8.4. Report findings to senior management.

---

## ESCALATION CONTACTS

| Role | Name | Contact |
|------|------|---------|
| Data Protection Officer | {{ dpo_name | default("[To Be Appointed]") }} | {{ contact_email }} |
| Grievance Officer | {{ dpo_name | default("[To Be Appointed]") }} | {{ grievance_email | default(contact_email) }} |
| Data Protection Board | — | [Board Portal] |

---

*This SOP must be reviewed quarterly and tested through tabletop exercises at least once annually.*
```
